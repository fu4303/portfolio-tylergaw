I’m a Designer

The number one most important thing to know about me as a designer: I give a shit.

Caring about what I’m designing–and more importantly the people that are going to use what I’m designing–is what makes me an great designer. I always design for an audience (if you’re reading this–tag–you’re it) and they are on my mind during the entire design process. My goal is not always to give the people what they want. Sometimes it is. Sometimes I want to challenge them. Sometimes I want to push them to behave in a certain way, to learn something new or to forget something old. Sometimes I want to make people laugh. Sometimes I want them to focus other times I’m trying to distract.

Design to me is about influencing human behavior. I use my skills to encourage, engage, entice, entertain, entangle, envelop, and enrage.

It’s hard to call yourself a Designer these days without qualifying it. I may not have held all of these official titles, but I have done and do the following types of design: Product, Web, Graphic, UX, UI, Interaction, Motion, and some others that I forget. None of those are distinct silos. The lines between each are blurry and they can happen indepently or all at once.

Quality Under Pressure

It’s paramount to get designed work out into the world. Deadlines are real, things need to be done yesterday. However, there is always time enough to pay attention to the details and push to make quality products. There will always be things that don’t quite get to the level we want, but there is never an excuse for entering a project with the mindset of doing the bare minimum. I do not and will not do that.

I’m a Developer

The number one most important thing to know about me as a developer: I give a shit.

Early on in my career I realized that there was nearly nothing that could not be done on the Web with code. From the beginning I’ve been blown away at what can be done with the vast array of tools I have access to as a developer. Every time a build something I learn something new regardless of the language or project size. I stay curious and open to new languages, frameworks, practices. While I have preferences, I do my best to avoid attachment to any language or style of writing code.

What does programming mean to me?

Learning to program has been a constant reminder that nothing comes easy. In my first programming classes I asked questions that made the other students’ eyes roll and the instructor to pause in confusion. When I first start I understood so little I could hardly misunderstand. Not much has changed since then. I’m just familiar with code. Comfortable with code. Each language has it’s own sytaxes and indiosycraties, but they all share commonalities. I latch on to those. I need those to pull myself into new languages and frameworks. Writing code is still magic to me. I hope I never know enough to make that untrue.

While I’m interested in programming as a craft, my main motivation for continuing to develop my skills is to build things. For me code will always be a means to an end. I have ideas and I want to see them come to life. Code–of all different shapes and sizes–is often needed to make those ideas reality.

What can I do for you?

I can design and build incredibly high-quality web sites/apps for you. I’m most skilled with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I know those like the back of my hand and have made great things with them.

On the server-side, I’ve built sites and apps using Node.js, PHP and Ruby on Rails. I know enough Python and Django to be dangerous.

I can design iPhone and iPad apps. I’m an iOS user and enjoy designing apps that follow the best practices and patterns put forth by Apple while bending and breaking those practices to create unexpectedly delightful details.
